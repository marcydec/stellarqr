Cosmogony

THE CREATION OF THE WORLD FROM CHAOS INTO MYTH

According to Greek mythology, however, in the beginning there was Chaos, that is, a universal and disordered mixture of matter; however, this disorder was a divinity capable of generating: it is Cosmogony. Most of the children of Chaos were capricious and malevolent deities. According to the cosmogony of Greek mythology, Destiny or Fate, now benign, now hostile, was born from Chaos. Then many other deities were born: Erebus, a kind of bottomless abyss; the Night, also dark, but the bearer of rest and advice; le Moire, ministers of Destiny; discord and old age. Later more forgiving divinities were born: Concordia, Love, Day, Uranus (i.e. Heaven) and Gea (i.e. Earth). Thus, by the will of Love, Night and Day, Concord and Discord, Heaven and Earth, formed personalities distinct from the others, ceasing to be the mixture of Chaos. Thus, from Chaos (disorder) the Cosmos (order) began to take shape: cosmogony is now in its advanced state. Uranus is the first god who reigned over the Universe as soon as it emerged from the chaos of Chaos. He married Gea and they had several children. Among them, the main ones are the Titans (including Oceanus and Cronos) and the Cyclopes.
